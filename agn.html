HETDEX

HETDEX (Gebhardt et al. 2021) is an ongoing spectroscopic survey on the upgraded 10 m Hobby–Eberly Telescope (HET) at McDonald Observatory in west Texas, USA. It uses the Visible Integral field Replicable Unit Spectrograph (VIRUS; Hill et al. 2021) to record spectra of every object falling within its field of view. A typical exposure contains 34,944 spectra, most of which capture “blank” sky. The primary goal of this survey is to measure the large-scale structure at z ∼ 3 using Lyα emitters (LAEs) as tracers. The HETDEX survey is expected to be active from 2017 to 2024, and eventually will cover 540 deg2 with a filling factor of 1 in 4.6.

The first AGN catalog in HETDEX

HETDEX provides the spectral observations of emission-line objects with no target preselection within 3500–5500 Å. Therefore, the AGN selection is based solely on their spectral features, free from any photometric preselection (magnitudes/colors/morphologies). Both luminous quasars and low-luminosity Seyferts are found in our catalog.

Figure: Visibility of strong AGN emission lines as a function of the redshift in the wavelength range of the HETDEX survey. The gray shaded region at z=[0.96-1.26] shows one representative redshift range where only one emission is visible.

The first AGN catalog ( Liu et al. 2022, ApJS, 261, 24) can be found here. It includes 5,322 AGN (z=[0.25-4.2]) identified from HETDEX observations from 2017 January 1 to 2020 June 26, covering an effective area of 30.61 deg2.

The North Ecliptic Pole (NEP) field (O. A. Chavez Ortiz et al. 2022, in preparation) and the other field were taken by our collaborators with their own scientific purposes.

Sample Selection

AGN candidates are selected either with at least two significant AGN emission lines, such as the Lyα and C IV λ1549 line pair, or with a single broad emission line with FWHM greater than 1000 km s−1. Each source is further confirmed by visual inspections.
